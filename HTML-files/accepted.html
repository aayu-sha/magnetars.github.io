Classifying Primary Cosmic Ray Composition using Machine Learning and Deep Learning Models

Roy, A., Singh, A. (2025). RAP 2025, Greece (March 2025, Oral Presentation).

View Acceptance Screenshot View Abstract

Abstract

Investigating the composition of initial cosmic rays is essential for comprehending the characteristics of high-energy cosmic phenomena and their astrophysical origins. Determining the predominant particle types such as protons, helium, and iron nuclei yields insights regarding cosmic accelerators and the fundamental physics that dictate their interactions with the interstellar medium. Traditional classification approaches rely on physics-based models and empirical parameterizations. However, issues such as noise, atmospheric disturbances, and lack of sufficient data often restrict the effectiveness of these methods. This study investigates the use of machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) models in order to classify primary cosmic rays based on key features such as energy, arrival direction, and shower particle statistics. Models like XGBoost, Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP), and TabTransformer's performance are assessed using empirical cosmic ray event data obtained from extensive air shower (EAS) measurements. The assessment results in the enhancement of the classification accuracy of the machine learning models significantly, with XGBoost achieving an accuracy of 94.57%. These findings do emphasise the potential of the AI-driven approaches in advancing high-energy astrophysics by improving the cosmic ray composition analysis and enabling more precise identification of their sources.

Formation and Evolution of Galactic Bars: Examining the Influence of Angular Momentum Transfer in Spiral Galaxies

Singh, A., Roy, A. (2025). 56th Division on Dynamical Astronomy Meeting, Atlanta, Georgia, USA (February 2025).

Abstract Acceptance View Abstract

Abstract

Galactic bars are essential dynamical structures that significantly influence the secular evolution of disk galaxies. Their genesis is influenced by gravitational instabilities, whereas their long-term evolution is regulated by the transfer of angular momentum among the stellar disk, dark matter halo, and central bulge. This research utilizes a mathematical and computational framework to examine the dynamic mechanisms underlying bar generation and evolution. The stability criteria for bar growth are analyzed using perturbation theory, resonance analysis, and numerical simulations derived from the collision-less Boltzmann equation and N-body methods. Significant emphasis is placed on the function of resonant angular momentum redistribution, encompassing co-rotation and Lindblad resonances, in governing bar strength and duration. The findings determine the impact of bars on galactic morphology, star formation, and central activity, enhancing the comprehension of secular galaxy evolution.
