Investigating the Behavior of Dark Matter-Analogous Particles in Microgravity Environments: A Hypothetical Study

Figure 1: Simulation of dark matter particle in microgravity: trajectory and speed evolution.

Figure 2: N-body simulation of two dark matter particles orbiting a baryonic center.

Figure 3: Comparison of particle trajectories in microgravity vs. Earth's gravity.

Simulation Results

This section includes graphs and images simulated for the paper, exploring the behavior of dark matter-analogous particles in microgravity.

  • Figure 1: Simulation of a dark matter particle in a microgravity environment (m = 1×10⁻²⁶ kg, g = 9.8×10⁻⁶ m/s², k = 1×10⁻³⁰ N/(m/s)), showing the particle's trajectory in the xy-plane with a downward curve and the time evolution of its speed (mm/s), which decreases initially and then gradually increases due to gravitational acceleration.
  • Figure 2: N-body simulation in microgravity (Python, NumPy), showing two particles (masses 100 GeV, initial velocities 0.02 m/s) orbiting a baryonic center over 500 time steps using a leapfrog integrator. The blue trajectory forms a near-circular orbit, while the orange trajectory is elongated, indicating enhanced weak interactions.
  • Figure 3: Comparative simulation of dark matter-analogous particle trajectories (Python, NumPy, SciPy) for axions (m = 10⁻²² eV) and WIMPs (m = 100 GeV) with initial velocities of 0.01 m/s over 1000 time steps. The image compares trajectories in microgravity (showing deviations due to enhanced weak interactions) and under Earth's gravity (where effects are suppressed).
